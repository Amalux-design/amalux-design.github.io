TrailBack — Hiker's Navigation App — Case Study

End-to-end process: research, design, and usability testing of an offline-first hiking navigation app.

Project Overview

Design a navigation app that records unmapped routes using GPS and guides users back to their starting point. Allow pinning of hazards and key spots — providing reliable offline navigation when internet is unavailable.

Problem

Many hikers get lost due to unreliable offline navigation and cluttered interfaces. TrailBack focuses on offline GPS tracing, simple UI, and safety features to reduce search & rescue incidents.

The Purpose

  • Record and retrace journeys offline using GPS on unmapped trails.
  • Mark and share key locations to alert fellow hikers about potential dangers.
  • Provide simple, reliable navigation tools with real-time route updates.

Research

Primary Research

Gathered qualitative feedback from hiking communities and mapped pain points: offline reliability, UI complexity, and safety gaps.

Competitor Analysis

Analyzed AllTrails, Gaia GPS, and Komoot. Many require internet or paid offline packs — opportunity for an accessible offline-first app.

Target Users

Hikers and outdoor explorers who need dependable offline navigation and lightweight safety features.

Pain Points

Unreliable offline maps, confusing visual density, and difficulty reaching safety controls.

User Journey Mapping

Mapping helped identify where users get lost or distracted and which controls need to be within thumb reach.

Key Insights

Implemented a minimalist satellite-first UI, removed non-essential widgets, relaunched clearer icons, made SOS prominent, and moved live telemetry (distance/elevation) to a bottom panel to reduce clutter.

Usability Testing & Onboarding

Short study with 4 participants: preference for short, direct routes and minimal, reachable controls. Iterations improved clarity and ease-of-use.

Onboarding

All 4 participants hesitated to sign up — added a clear "Skip Sign In" option on the welcome screen to reduce entry friction.

Navigation

Participants preferred a conventional satellite map. We removed weather/compass widgets that added clutter and could fail offline. SOS is now placed prominently; telemetry moved to a bottom shutter for a cleaner map view.

Marker & SOS

Marker

The marker lets users pin hazards or points of interest and optionally share them with fellow hikers.

SOS

The SOS button quickly alerts emergency contacts with coordinates. It can use phone sensors to detect sudden movements and trigger alerts when appropriate.

Learning and Insights

TrailBack taught that empathy and simplicity are key. The app works silently in the background, uses minimal resources, and records trails accurately using GPS so users can focus on the journey.
